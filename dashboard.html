    Backtest & validation

    Validate the rules engine against historical data before trusting live results with full capital.

    Run backtest locally
    1. Open your terminal in the investment-system folder
    python3 -m v4.intelligence.backtest --start 2020-01-01 --end 2025-01-01
    2. Test different periods:
    python3 -m v4.intelligence.backtest --start 2022-01-01 --end 2023-01-01
    Tests industry ETF momentum rotation vs SPY buy-and-hold with 0.1% transaction costs. Results saved to data/backtest/
    Portfolio Visualizer (no-code)
    1. Go to portfoliovisualizer.com
    2. Select Backtest Portfolio
    3. Set dates: Jan 2019 → Dec 2024
    4. Portfolio A (Your strategy): SOXX 34%, IGV 33%, CIBR 33%
    5. Portfolio B (Benchmark): SPY 100%
    6. Set rebalancing: Quarterly
    7. Compare results — look for positive alpha and acceptable drawdown
    This tests whether top-performing industry ETFs historically beat SPY — the core assumption of your strategy.
    What to look for in results
    ✅ Green flags
    • Alpha > 0% vs SPY
    • CAGR beats SPY in 3+ of 5 tested periods
    • Max drawdown < 35%
    • Works in both bull and bear markets
    ⚠️ Yellow flags
    • Only beats SPY in bull markets
    • Alpha < 2% (fees may erase it)
    • Works in 2 of 5 periods
    • High drawdown (> 40%)
    🔴 Red flags
    • Consistently underperforms SPY
    • Only works in 1 period
    • Max drawdown > 50%
    • Returns disappear after costs

    Now
    Run Portfolio Visualizer backtest across 2019-2024. If positive alpha, proceed with small capital deployment.
    6-8 weeks
    Log every signal with full conviction breakdown. Track vs SPY daily. Win rate tracker starts accumulating data.
    3-4 months
    First kill criteria review. Is active equity outperforming SPY? If yes, increase allocation. If not, pause new entries.
    100+ trades
    First conviction weight update. Component attribution analysis tells you which of the 7 signals actually predicted winners.
