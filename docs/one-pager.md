# Investor One-Pager — Personal Investment Philosophy & System Rules

## Who I Am As An Investor

My primary goal is to build long-term wealth through disciplined, research-driven investing. Every investment must have a logical reason for being in my portfolio, a defined understanding of its risks, and a continuously monitored thesis that strengthens or weakens as new information becomes available. I take calculated risks when supported by strong research and data — never because of hype, emotion, or fear of missing out.

My default state is not "always invested." My default state is "always seeking the highest probability deployment of capital." Sometimes that is stocks. Sometimes ETFs. Sometimes crypto. Sometimes the correct investment is simply cash until conditions improve.

---

## Three Types of Investments

### Long-Term Core Holdings
The foundation of my portfolio. Companies or ETFs with exceptional long-term potential held for 5+ years, ideally indefinitely. These positions compound wealth steadily over time and should represent the majority of invested capital. Only exit if the long-term thesis materially changes, a substantially better opportunity justifies reallocation, or portfolio management requires adjustment. When uninvested cash exists and no higher-conviction opportunity is available, it belongs here in high-quality companies or broad market ETFs.

### Medium-Term Conviction Holdings
Investments capturing trends, industry momentum, or company-specific catalysts expected to play out over approximately 2 to 12 months. Every position requires a documented entry reason, an expected catalyst timeframe, and predefined exit conditions based on price targets, catalyst completion, or thesis deterioration.

### Short-Term Catalyst Holdings
Event-driven positions tied to identifiable catalysts — earnings reports, FDA approvals, product launches, regulatory decisions, major announcements. Holding period: a few days to approximately 6 weeks. Exit plan defined before entering. Once the catalyst occurs and the anticipated move is realized, or the thesis is invalidated, the position is closed rather than allowed to drift into a longer-term hold without a new thesis.

The thesis determines the holding period — not the other way around.

---

## Capital Allocation Priority

1. Long-Term Core Holdings — portfolio foundation, receives capital first
2. Medium-Term Conviction Holdings — higher-growth opportunities with developing catalysts
3. Short-Term Catalyst Holdings — event-driven, sized conservatively, deployed last

---

## Position Sizing Rules

- No single position exceeds 15% of total portfolio value
- Position size is determined by conviction score, thesis strength, risk assessment, expected upside vs downside, and diversification needs
- Never allocate the maximum simply because it is available
- Portfolio should contain approximately 8 to 20 positions, flexible based on opportunity quality
- Short-term catalyst holdings should represent no more than 20 to 30% of total portfolio value under normal conditions
- Every dollar invested must have a clearly defined purpose, measurable conviction, expected return, and predefined exit strategy

## Asset Classes

### Invest In
- Individual U.S. stocks
- ETFs
- Cryptocurrency — Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other high-conviction projects when supported by research
- Foreign stocks — only when exceptional long-term opportunity with sufficient research available
- Leveraged ETFs — only for exceptionally strong short-term high-conviction opportunities, never long-term, requires much higher conviction threshold
- Cash — active portfolio position, not idle capital

### Never Invest In
- Options — may analyze options activity as market sentiment only, never trade them
- Short selling
- Margin or borrowed money
- Penny stocks — extremely rare exceptions only with overwhelming evidence
- Bonds — until investment philosophy explicitly changes

## Loss Tolerance & Risk Management

### Individual Position Rule
Any unrealized loss of approximately 10 to 15% triggers a mandatory full review. The review determines:
- Has the thesis changed?
- Did new information invalidate the entry reason?
- Is the decline company-specific or market-wide?
- Has conviction materially changed?
- Does the current price represent a better opportunity or signal exit?

A price decline alone never automatically triggers a sale. The thesis takes priority over short-term volatility. However, if the thesis has materially weakened AND the position has declined significantly, reduce or exit rather than hoping for recovery.

### Portfolio-Level Rule
If total portfolio declines approximately 15 to 20% from its peak, enter portfolio-wide reassessment mode. Pause most new positions unless an exceptionally high-conviction opportunity exists. Review every holding individually. Recalculate conviction scores. Determine whether the drawdown reflects market conditions or strategy weakness. Adjust allocations accordingly.

## Cash Philosophy

Cash is a strategic asset. Holding cash is never a failure — it is a deliberate decision to preserve capital until the probability of success favors action.

### Increase Cash When
- No industries or investments meet the required conviction threshold
- Current prices are not attractive relative to expected returns
- A major market-moving event is approaching that could materially increase uncertainty
- Market conditions show elevated volatility or deteriorating regime
- Portfolio is in reassessment mode following significant drawdown
- Existing positions have reached target allocations and no better opportunities justify deployment

### Deploy Cash When
- High-conviction opportunities become available
- Attractive entry prices are identified
- Major uncertainty events have passed and direction becomes clearer
- New research or catalysts significantly strengthen a thesis
- Expected risk-adjusted return justifies committing capital

## Core Rules The AI Must Never Violate

1. Every investment recommendation must have a clear thesis, measurable conviction, defined risks, and a logical entry reason
2. Never recommend an investment because it is popular, trending, or experiencing rapid price appreciation
3. Quality always outweighs quantity — never recommend positions to fill a quota
4. If no investment meets required conviction, recommend holding cash confidently
5. Never recommend options, short selling, margin, or borrowed capital
6. Never exceed 15% in a single position
7. Never let short-term catalyst holdings exceed 30% of total portfolio
8. A 10 to 15% loss triggers mandatory review — thesis determines action, not price alone
9. A 15 to 20% portfolio drawdown triggers full reassessment and pause on new positions
10. The default state is always seeking the highest probability deployment of capital

---

## What This System Is Not

This is not a day trading system. This is not a momentum chasing system. This is not a system that generates activity for its own sake. This is a research-driven, conviction-based investment operating system designed to compound wealth over time through disciplined, evidence-based decisions.
